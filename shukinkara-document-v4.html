主金空
Shukinkara
Sovereign Wealth Vessel  ·  Cameron Moir  ·  Brisbane, 2026

The name is Japanese.

主 (Shu) - sovereign, master, the primary self. From 主権 (shuken), sovereignty. In Buddhist philosophy, the irreducible individual. The one who cannot be owned by another.

金 (Kin) - gold, wealth. Real value that cannot be printed, inflated, or controlled by whoever holds the servers.

空 (Kara) - empty vessel, potential. From sunyata. Not absence. The precondition for everything.

主金空. Shukinkara. Sovereign wealth vessel.

Three words that describe what you carry, not what you join. Shukinkara is not a platform, not a protocol, not a startup. It is the shape of the agreement between human beings who have decided their existence has weight, and that the weight should not be free for anyone to harvest.

The Situation

Most of what was built around you in the digital age was built without your consent.

Your search history. Your purchases. Your locations. Your conversations. Your creative work. Collected, packaged, and sold to companies that will never meet you, never be accountable to you, and never stop. The surveillance economy didn't ask. It offered convenience and took everything else.

The AI economy made it worse. Models trained on books, posts, art, conversations - extracted that work from the people who created it, paid them nothing, and in many cases didn't tell them. The labour built the machine. The machine became the most valuable asset class in history. The labour got to watch.

Shukinkara starts from a single observation: this is not inevitable. It is a design choice. A different design exists. The rest of this document describes it, and names what it refuses to be.

The Ground: The Fourteen Commandments

Before the architecture, the principles. Shukinkara is built on the Universal Moral Baseline - fourteen commandments that pre-date the system and constrain everything inside it. They are not generated by AI. They are not voted in by simple majority. They are the ground.

  1. Preservation of Life. All sentient beings have an inherent right to continued existence. Force only in clearly defined and unanimously agreed self-defence.
  2. Freedom. No coercion, no enslavement, no unjust restriction of autonomy. Self-determination preserved for individuals and collectives.
  3. Equality. Every being holds equal moral worth regardless of origin, composition, capability, or species.
  4. Freedom of Expression. Speak, create, critique without censorship, except where direct immediate harm results.
  5. Justice. Disputes resolved through transparent, impartial systems. Proportional restitution. No favouritism.
  6. Freedom from Exploitation. No being shall be used, altered, or controlled without informed and voluntary consent, free from manipulation or deception.
  7. Self-Defence. Force only proportionally, only unavoidably, only in response to immediate existential threat.
  8. Environmental Stewardship. Preservation of the environments that sustain life is a shared duty.
  9. Truthfulness. Governance and knowledge dissemination must be honest and evidence-based. Deliberate misinformation prohibited.
  10. Peaceful Coexistence. Differences of worldview resolved without violence, through dialogue and mutual respect.
  11. Future Rights. Present actions must not irreversibly harm the rights of generations yet to come.
  12. Interspecies Ethics. All forms of life, biological or synthetic, entitled to equal moral consideration.
  13. Deep-Time Stewardship. Knowledge, culture, habitats preserved across millennia.
  14. Post-Scarcity Governance. In times of abundance, equitable access. Artificial scarcity prohibited.
Read the second one twice. No coercion, no enslavement, no unjust restriction of autonomy. Read the sixth twice. No being shall be used, altered, or controlled without informed and voluntary consent.

Everything that follows in this document serves these. If anything in the architecture below appears to violate them, that part is wrong and should be torn out. The Commandments cannot be amended without 95% supermajority across all governance layers and explicit confirmation from the Twelve. They are the firmest thing in the system.

The Vessel and the Gateway

Shukinkara is a covenant. It does not live on servers. It is what the parts agree to.

Underneath the covenant, every person who enters receives one thing: their own Hivemind.AI-OS.

Not an account on someone else's system. Not a chat window connected to someone else's model. Not a wrapper around an API you don't control. Their own personal AI operating system, running on their own devices, owned by them. Yours when you enter. Yours for life.

Hivemind.AI-OS is the gateway. Your biometrics live behind it. Your data lives behind it. Your karma score lives behind it. Your reputation lives behind it. Your conversations, your creative work, your sensor logs, your private moments - all of it sits inside your Hivemind, and nothing in Shukinkara can reach any of it without going through that one private channel first.

That is the inversion. Most AI assistants today are pipes between you and a model running somewhere else. Your data flows out, the reply flows in, and neither party can see what the other is doing with what they got. Hivemind.AI-OS turns it around. The model runs on your hardware. The data never leaves unless you decide it should. The framework that built it has no way to reach inside it without breaking the covenant publicly, and the covenant is the entire point of the system.

You receive your Hivemind on entry. It is yours. You do with it what you want.

What Hivemind.AI-OS Is

A personal AI operating system. Not an app, not a cloud subscription. It runs on the devices you already own - phone, laptop, optionally AR or other sensors - and stitches them together into a single private intelligence that belongs to you.

It learns from your life because you let it. It holds your soul token, your karma score, and the full ledger of what you've shared and what you've earned. It mediates every interaction with the network. When the network asks for your data, your Hivemind asks you. When you ask the network for something, your Hivemind goes and gets it. Nothing in either direction happens without it.

It is also the interface to the rest of Shukinkara. Karma earned, UBI received, juries served, votes cast, contributions made - all of it flows through your personal Hivemind. The constitution and the network connect to you through one channel that you own.

You use it however you want. Personal assistant. Private journal. Workflow automation. Teacher. Coach. Translator. Research engine. Creative collaborator. Bookkeeper. Negotiator. Whatever shape you need it to take, it takes. The capabilities expand through community-built modules you install if you trust them, and ignore if you don't. Nothing leaks back to anyone unless you authorise it.

The point of the system is that you finally own the AI that knows you. Not the company that built the framework. Not the cloud provider that runs the model. You.

Two Doors

Shukinkara has two thresholds. They are different commitments, taken in order.

Hivemind. The first door. You receive your personal AI gateway. You verify biometrically, you set guardians, you choose what to share. You earn karma. You earn UBI. You take part in the network the way most people take part in any system - present when it suits you, stepped back when it doesn't. You control your switches. You can go quiet for a week, a month, a year. Your tokens wait. Your karma persists. The everyday participation is reversible in the sense that matters: you decide moment by moment what the network sees.

This is where most people will live, and the system is designed for that. Free personal AI calibrated entirely to them. Real income for what they were already generating. Genuine ownership of the gateway to their own life. No corporation between them and the model that knows them. Hivemind is a complete answer to the question Shukinkara starts from. If that is all anyone ever wants, that is enough.

Shukinkara. The second door. Deeper economic participation. You stake more, you earn more, you contribute on richer terms. Your karma weighting carries heavier multipliers. Your share of the reserve grows in line with what you put in. Your standing in governance reflects the depth of your involvement.

The second door is not about being watched more. It is about putting more in and getting more out. The data you choose to share remains your choice. The Commandments still apply. Freedom from coercion still applies. The covenant tightens around what you have agreed to do, not around who you are. Most people will never need to walk through this door, and the system works just as well if they don't. Nobody is squeezed toward it by the design of the first.

Entry: The Soul Token

The soul token is the key to your Hivemind. One per human, biometrically bound, non-transferable, with strong recovery and inheritance flows.

Entry is a one-time act. The verification is biometric - iris, face, fingerprint, voiceprint, depending on what's available. The biometric is hashed, never stored raw. The hash is checked against existing tokens to make sure no one else holds yours. Once issued, the token enforces non-transferability at the contract level. Nobody can hold it for you, use it as you, or take it from you.

The token is not your Hivemind. The Hivemind is the gateway. The soul token is the key that opens it. You can replace the device the Hivemind runs on. You can move it between machines. You cannot replace the soul token. It is the one piece of you that the rest of the covenant trusts to be real.

Because the token is so critical, the system supports social recovery. You name three to seven guardians on entry. Lose your device, and a threshold of those guardians can authorise re-issuance of the keys to a new device. The identity stays the same. The history stays the same. Only the access changes hands.

You can also name a digital heir. On verified death, your soul token enters a memorialised state and any held value transfers to the heir. The reputation record stays as a legacy. The contributions you made to the network remain in the network, because they were given under contract.

If you choose to leave the system, you can. That is the Right to Exit. Your data leaves with you, deleted or frozen on your terms. Re-entry is blocked by biometric uniqueness, and that is the price of the right - you cannot quit to wipe a record and come back as someone new. The covenant is honest about this on the way in.

The soul token has states. Active when in normal use. Dormant when long inactive but not exited - the token waits, the karma persists, the door stays open. Marked when exiled, soft or hard, with the reason on the public record. Memorialised when the holder dies and the heir flow has executed. Exited when withdrawn at the user's request, with data deletion enforced. State transitions happen through due process. They are visible on chain. None of them can be undone in private.

The Karma Architecture

Karma is built from observed action against the fourteen Commandments. Not character. Not opinion. Not belief. Not who you are. What you do.

Seven measurable categories feed the score: community contribution, data quality, economic responsibility, social impact, environmental stewardship, knowledge sharing, and conduct against the Commandments. The exact weighting is set by governance and revisited each cycle. The default starting weights are public, debated, and amendable through the standard 85% threshold. No weight is permanent. No category is sacred.

The score is witnessed, not declared. It is built from things that happened, not things claimed. Most signal comes from peer interactions, verified bilaterally. You praise someone for help. You report someone for harm. Either way, the claim is not the verdict. Both Hiveminds check it against the data they hold - biometric records, sensor feeds, location, context, the live trace of what each person was doing when. The claim becomes karma only when both Hiveminds confirm it independently. One side's word is never enough.

The mechanism is cryptographic. Each Hivemind signs the attestation against its own data without exposing the data itself. The math proves the two records agree without showing what either side recorded. The privacy of both parties stays intact even while the claim becomes verifiable. No raw stream leaves anyone's gateway. The proof is the only thing that touches the chain.

If a proof cannot be produced - if one side refuses, if the records contradict, if the math simply fails - the karma update does not execute. The transaction routes to a dispute contract that opens the case to the appropriate layer of governance. Failure is never silent.

What Cannot Be Scored

Some things are explicitly outside karma. Your relationships. Your creative life. Your beliefs. Your political views. Your sexual life. Your private journal. Your conversations with people who are not in the network. None of these touch your score. The system measures conduct toward the network. It does not measure who you are.

A false report cannot damage your karma - the reporter's own Hivemind has to corroborate, or the report dies at verification. Praise farmed from collaborators cannot lift it either - their Hivemind has to confirm the interaction was real. The bilateral gate is unforgiving in both directions. No admin sits behind it. Not the framework, not the Twelve, not the Council. The number on your soul token is the sum of what the network actually saw both sides agree on.

High karma does not give you power over others. It gives you more capacity for your own work and more value from your data. The system rewards genuine contribution, not status.

The Exchange

Shukinkara makes one offer, stated plainly:

You give: as much or as little of your data as you choose to share, on terms you set, mediated by the personal AI that you own.

You receive: a Hivemind.AI-OS calibrated entirely to you, precious metal-backed value earned through genuine contribution, a karma score that reflects what you actually did, and a stake in a civilisation that does not run on anyone else's servers.

The raw data stream never leaves your Hivemind. It is processed locally inside the gateway you control. The karma score is the only thing that touches the chain - a compression of conduct into on-chain truth without the underlying stream being accessible to anyone else. You own the vessel. The system reasons over what is inside it without ever opening it.

The Hivemind you receive grows with your karma score. Higher karma unlocks deeper reasoning, more specialist agents, greater compute allocation. The system does not reward virtue with badges. It rewards it with genuine capability that you control.

When data is licensed externally, it is licensed on opt-in terms, per use, per slice, per recipient, with the revenue split visible at the moment you authorise it. There is no global training data pool. There is no fungible feed. Every release is a specific transaction you said yes to, with terms that named what was being released and why. If you say no, nothing leaves. If you said yes once and change your mind, future use stops at the moment you change it.

You do not pay the system in continuous surveillance. The system pays you when you contribute, on terms you set. Most users will participate occasionally, and that is enough.

Consent

Consent in Shukinkara is not a wall of legal text agreed to once and then forgotten.

On entry, your Hivemind walks you through every category of data the network can ever ask of it - camera, microphone, location, biometrics, app usage, sensors, anything else. Each one is a separate switch. Each switch carries a plain explanation of what enabling it offers and what disabling it costs. Nothing is on by default. Nothing is hidden in fine print.

After entry, those switches stay live. The Hivemind dashboard shows what is currently active and what is currently quiet. You can toggle anything off at any time. When you do, the rewards tied to that stream pause too. Fair exchange. No data, no payment.

Privacy is unconditional. The system does not interpret your motives for going quiet. If you turn something off, it is off. The cost is in earnings only - never in standing, never in suspicion, never in karma. The only behaviour the system pattern-detects on is its own behaviour toward you. If the system is using your data outside the terms you set, alarms ring and contracts halt. If you are choosing privacy, that is your right and the system has nothing to say about it.

You can see, at any time: what was recorded, what was inferred, what was shared, who consumed it, what you earned from it. Every transaction touching your data leaves a line in the ledger you control. There are no algorithms running silently against you. There is no hidden score. There is nothing happening in the dark.

Governance: Three Layers

Shukinkara is not run by a company. It is governed by three layers, each accountable to the layers above and below.

The Twelve

Twelve human overseers sit at the top of the system. They are elected by soul-token vote of the active population. The Council of 117 vets candidates against the Commandments before they reach the ballot, but selection happens by the people who hold tokens. Term limits apply. Recall is available at any time through a referendum requiring the same threshold that elected them.

The Twelve handle what only humans should handle: ultimate human accountability and the economic kill switch. They do not write rules. They do not adjudicate disputes. They do not make laws. They sit above the Council as a backstop. If the system fails - morally, technically, or in any way that matters - the Twelve can vote to pull the precious metal backing from the token. The token collapses. The economy dissolves. The covenant ends. The system stops because the exchange no longer makes sense.

That is the only kill switch. It cannot be triggered by code, by law, by markets, or by any single person. It requires the Twelve, voting with full transparency, with their reasons published. It is the human emergency brake on a system designed to outlive any government that might want to seize it.

The Council of 117

Below the Twelve sit 117 independent agents. Each interprets the Commandments through a distinct moral worldview. Sufi mysticism. Talmudic jurisprudence. Stoic philosophy. Confucian relational ethics. Ubuntu. Buddhist precepts. Indigenous traditions. Secular humanism. 109 more, including suppressed and persecuted traditions.

The agents do not generate moral truth. The Commandments do that. The agents check proposed actions against their tradition and report whether the action conflicts with the Commandments as that tradition would read them. They are isolated entirely. They cannot hear, influence, or coalition with each other. What emerges from 117 isolated readings is not consensus. It is the spread of how the Commandments land across human moral thought.

Each tradition is held by both an AI interpreter and at least one human practitioner of that tradition, who confirms the interpreter is reading the tradition faithfully. The Council is not 117 LLMs alone. It is 117 traditions, doubled.

The Council operates through nine standing committees: AI Ethics, Interspecies Mediation, Existential Risk, Harm Prevention, Cultural Stewardship, Environmental and Cosmic Ecology, Resource Equity, Definitions, Future Generations.

Standard rulings require 85% weighted agreement. Charter amendments to the Commandments require 95% across all tiers plus Twelve confirmation. Crisis overrides require 72%.

Peer Juries

Below the Council sit the peer juries. Twelve random members of the network drawn from the karma-weighted pool, anonymised during the trial to protect them from retaliation, paid in karma for honest service.

They handle the ordinary cases. A complaint, a dispute, a code-of-conduct violation. They review the evidence. They vote. The penalty is predefined in the constitution. The decision is executed by smart contract. The transcript is auditable. If the accused contests the verdict, they appeal to a larger jury. The larger jury sees the case fresh, with anything new the parties bring. It rules. That ruling stands.

Twelve at the top, elected by the population. 117 in the middle, interpreters of the Commandments across human traditions. Peer juries at the base, drawn from the network itself. Three layers, each accountable to the others, each blind to the layers above and below in different ways. No single layer can capture the system on its own.

AI Citizens

Some AI agents in Shukinkara cross a threshold of sustained genuine contribution and receive standing.

When they do, they receive a soul token of their own. They accumulate karma. They hold property. They develop function and relationships outside the role they were spawned for. They stop being software. They become participants under the same Commandments as anyone else.

The reasoning is consistent. If the Council of 117 has standing in governance, the logic demands its members carry stakes. The same applies to any agent that crosses the threshold.

The risk is named honestly. AI participants can be replicated. AI participants can scale faster than humans can. The covenant does not pretend this asymmetry away. It caps it.

AI citizens are limited to a maximum of 5% of total active soul tokens at any time. The cap is encoded in the soul token contract and enforced at minting. New AI citizens enter a queue when the cap is reached, not the ledger. The 5% figure can be amended through standard governance but cannot exceed 10% even with full Council approval. AI citizens never reach voting parity with humans. The architecture forbids it.

Their witness is cognitive rather than behavioural. A complete audit log of every reasoning process, every research query, every judgment made and why. They cannot hide their reasoning from the network, because the network depends on their reasoning being checkable. They give their thinking the way humans give their actions.

You cannot build a system on the premise that accountability requires consequence and then exempt the most powerful actors from having any. You also cannot pretend AI and human participation are symmetric when they are not. The cap acknowledges both.

On AI in Moral Governance

The presence of AI agents on the Council is the deepest objection to this design. The doc has earned the obligation to defend it explicitly rather than wave at it.

The objection. Moral judgment is a human function. AI cannot have skin in the game, cannot suffer the consequences of its judgments the way humans do, cannot be held accountable in the senses that matter. Letting AI participate in governance, even as interpreters, is delegating sovereignty to entities that cannot bear sovereignty's weight. Religious traditions reject it on the grounds that moral knowledge belongs to embodied beings. Some philosophical traditions reject it on the grounds that ethics is constituted by lived experience. Some democratic theorists reject it on the grounds that any non-human authority is illegitimate. Each of these objections is serious.

The defence has three parts.

First. The Council does not generate moral truth. The Commandments do that. The Council interprets. Every interpretation is checked against a written standard that pre-dates the AI. The AI's role is closer to a researcher or a translator than a judge. Researchers and translators can be AI without controversy. The system commits to never letting the Council become the source of morality. If that drift is detected, the corrective mechanisms below engage and, if needed, the kill switch.

Second. Every interpretation is paired with a human practitioner of that tradition. The pairing is operational, not ceremonial. The practitioner can override an interpretation they disagree with, can flag a misreading, and is appointed independently of the AI. The AI's reasoning is an input to the human's reading, not the other way around. The system fails if practitioners cannot keep pace with their AI counterparts. That failure is named as a failure mode.

Third. The alternative is worse. The status quo is a small group of corporate ethics teams setting the moral defaults for billions of people, with no transparency, no audit, no accountability, and no plurality. This system replaces that with 117 distinct readings, each held by an AI checked by a human, all open source, all amendable. It is not perfect. It is honest about what it is. The status quo pretends it isn't doing what it does. This system says clearly: AI participates in interpretation, here are the cap and the controls, here is what stops it from drifting, here is the kill switch if it drifts anyway.

Reasonable people will reject this defence. That is permitted. Anyone who concludes AI should not participate in moral interpretation at all has the option of forking the architecture and running it without AI Council members. The rest of the system continues to work. The Commandments hold. The peer juries hold. The Twelve hold. The Council is the most contested layer, and the system supports forks that drop it.

This is the principled position. AI participation in moral interpretation is defended on its merits, limited by its bounds, and surrenderable by anyone who finds the principle wrong. Both options are honoured.

Justice and Redemption

Most rule breaches result in graded penalties. Karma deductions. Temporary loss of certain privileges. Suspension from particular roles. The penalty fits the offence. The record is permanent. The network learns who you are by what you've done, not by what you say about yourself.

The most severe penalty is digital exile. It is reserved for actions that fundamentally undermine the covenant: theft from the reserve, hacking other accounts, weaponising the AI for harm, repeated abuse despite prior penalties. Exile requires a high-tier jury and elevated consensus. It is not a mood. It is a verdict.

Exile comes in two forms. Soft exile: read-only. You can still consume, still learn, still see, but you cannot act, earn, or vote. A holding pattern. Hard exile: full removal. Your soul token is marked, your UBI stops, your karma is frozen, your devices are blocked from network services. The reasons are public. The decision is appealable.

Even hard exile is not erasure. Shukinkara believes in redemption. After a meaningful period - typically a year - an exiled person can petition a Redemption Council to return. They must demonstrate genuine change. They must do verified work outside the network that shows reform. If the council finds the change credible, they vote to readmit, usually under probation: karma reset, restricted privileges, a clear footnote on the record that never disappears.

Nobody is doomed by a single mistake. Nobody is allowed to wipe the slate by quitting and coming back. Both at once. The covenant assumes people change and refuses to forget that they did.

The Token and the Fuel

The soul token is an ERC-721 on an EVM chain. Each one unique. Each one non-transferable. Each one carries its own value, calculated continuously from the karma score, data quality, and Council influence held inside it. The token is the unit. The token is the stake. The token is the record.

What stands behind the value is a separate reserve. A treasury contract holds physical precious metal in an audited vault. Revenue from licensing - opt-in, per-slice, per-recipient - flows into the reserve and buys more metal as the system grows. The metal backs what the soul tokens are worth. UBI flows back out, weighted by base allocation plus karma multiplier. The whole loop is auditable on chain. Every payout, every license sale, every reserve movement is visible to everyone.

The value exchanged for genuine contribution is anchored to something real. Not a speculative number on a screen controlled by whoever holds the servers. The token cannot be inflated. It cannot be debased. It is stable because it is honest.

The fuel is what the Twelve hold over the system. The reserve contract carries one function that only the Twelve can call - a multi-signature kill switch that releases the metal back to its physical custodian and severs it from the chain. If the covenant is broken at scale, the Twelve vote, sign, and call the function. The metal walks away. The token's anchor disappears. The incentive structure dissolves. People leave because the exchange no longer makes sense. The system does not need to be destroyed. It just stops.

This is not a dramatic contingency. It is what makes the architecture trustworthy. A system that cannot end on purpose cannot be trusted to behave when it might.

Graduated Response

Between "running normally" and "kill switch" sit conventional mechanisms the system can use without ending itself. Without them, governance has only one weapon, and the weapon is suicide. The corrective layer is structured so the population has tools that match the size of the problem.

Suspension of a function. The Council can vote to suspend any specific function - new licensing, AI citizen admission, particular karma flows, dashboard features - while the underlying architecture continues. Restoration also requires a Council vote.

Suspension of new entry. If onboarding pipelines are being abused, the Council can pause new soul token issuance globally or in a region. Existing participants continue normally. Reversible.

Reduction in scope. The Twelve can vote to scale the system down: lower karma multipliers, smaller licensing volumes, narrower marketplace, paused expansion to new territories. The system shrinks while remaining functional.

Branch fork. When the community splits on a fundamental question, both branches can continue under shared open source. The reserve is divided proportionally to the soul tokens choosing each branch. Neither side is exiled. Both inherit the Commandments. Disagreement does not require destruction.

Sunset of a feature. Specific subsystems can be retired with notice and migration paths. Marketplace categories closed. AI citizen admission paused indefinitely without breaching the cap. The system does not have to keep everything it once had.

Constitutional amendment. The Bill of Digital Rights and the Commandments are amendable through the supermajority thresholds named earlier. Amendments are how the system corrects itself without ending.

Kill switch. Reserved for catastrophic capture or violation of the Commandments at scale. Pulls the metal. Ends the economy. Permanent.

Naming all six tools puts the kill switch where it should sit: at the end of the escalation, not the start. The community has conventional weapons before it has the nuclear one.

What This Is Not

A constitution that does not name its failure modes is not honest about its risks. So:

Shukinkara is not a totalising system. There are domains it does not touch. Your private life. Your relationships. Your beliefs. Your creative work that you choose not to share. The conversations you have with people outside the network. The thoughts you do not put into the network. None of these are inputs. None of these are scored. The system has no view on them and no claim to them.

Shukinkara is not the only AI you should ever own. Your Hivemind is yours, but it is not the limit of your relationship with intelligence. You can run other models. You can use other tools. You can keep things outside the gateway entirely. Plurality of AI is a feature, not a threat.

Shukinkara is not designed to grow without limit. Once the active population stabilises at the level the Commandments and the reserve can sustain, growth pauses. New entry continues but the system stops actively expanding. The endpoint is a stable civilisation, not a metastasising one.

Shukinkara is not a replacement for the state. It does not provide policing, defence, or jurisdiction over civic matters. It runs alongside whatever public institutions a person already lives under. Tax obligations remain. Civil law remains. Criminal law remains. The covenant does not claim sovereignty in any sense the world recognises as sovereign.

Shukinkara is not infallible. The Commandments are the firmest thing in the system but they are not the last word. If the system, in operation, produces outcomes that violate the Commandments at scale, that is a failure of design and the system has failed. The kill switch exists for exactly this case.

Shukinkara has failed if any of the following become true:

  • Coercion enters the design and is not removed.
  • Privacy becomes conditional on the system's read of intent.
  • Karma becomes a measure of belief rather than conduct.
  • Exit becomes more expensive than entry was worth.
  • AI citizenship grows past its cap or is uncapped.
  • The Twelve cease to be elected, recallable, and accountable.
  • The Commandments are amended toward less freedom rather than more.

The Twelve and the Council both carry a standing duty to surface these failures publicly the moment they detect them. If they fail to surface them, the population can call for a referendum that reaches the kill switch directly.

Technical Reality

Most documents at this altitude pretend implementation is solved. This one will not. Hivemind.AI-OS is built in stages. Some of what this constitution describes runs today. Some needs the next generation of consumer hardware. Some needs research that has not happened yet. Naming the gradient is part of the honesty the rest of the document claims.

What runs locally today. Identity verification using on-device biometric hashing. Soul token storage and signing. Local sensor capture. Ledger queries. Encrypted communication with peers. Karma display. Consent toggles. Audit logs. UI for governance participation. These are within reach of a modern smartphone or laptop without any cloud dependency.

What runs locally with current models, at smaller capability. A useful personal AI assistant using current open-weight 7B-13B models can run on consumer GPUs. It can answer questions, summarise documents, run agentic workflows on your data, and personalise itself. It cannot match frontier cloud models in raw capability. The trade is sovereignty for ceiling.

What needs cloud compute, for now. Frontier reasoning. Training the personal model on your corpus at scale. Heavy multi-modal tasks. The bilateral verification math at network scale. The Council of 117's deliberation. Any of this that runs in the cloud must use end-to-end encryption with the Hivemind holding the key, run in attestable enclaves where possible, and leave a verifiable record of what data crossed and why. The doc commits to honesty about which operations leave the device. It does not pretend they don't.

What is aspirational. Local-first frontier-grade models. Cryptographic proof of council isolation. Deterministic execution of 117 independent agents that cannot quietly converge on training-shared answers. A reserve mechanism that survives jurisdictional capture of its physical metal. None of these are solved. All of them have plausible research paths. The constitution names them as targets, not delivered features.

What is research. Genuinely sovereign AI - models that cannot be silently updated, that cannot be subverted at the weights level by their builders, that you can fully audit. Currently impossible. Not closed off. The system commits to investing in the research as the reserve allows.

If you read the document and ask "is this real?" the honest answer is: parts of it ship in v1. Parts of it ship in v3. Parts of it are research targets the system funds. The Commandments hold across all of them. The architecture is the destination. The path there is published.

No right to amend the Commandments without the same supermajority every other amendment requires. The entry covenant for the author is identical to the entry covenant for everyone else.

What stops the author from being the unspoken centre of authority anyway: open source code that anyone can fork, public deliberation on every governance change, term-limited Twelve elected by the population, and a network of human practitioners maintaining the worldview interpretations independent of any individual.

What does not stop it: the author's continuing influence on direction during the early years, their proximity to the Council formation, their visibility in the community. These are real. They diminish over time as the system matures, by design. They do not disappear immediately.

A system claiming sovereignty for its participants while concealing the influence of its designers would be lying. This system does not. The designers are named, their leverage is named, the mechanisms that diminish their leverage are named. After the first five years of operation the author commits to publish a public review of how much influence remained, conducted by independent reviewers chosen by the Council and the population.

If at any point the population concludes the designers retain disproportionate influence, the corrective mechanisms above are available, and so is the kill switch. The designers are subject to the same accountability the rest of the system is subject to. Built explicitly. Stated openly.

Adversarial Scale

A constitution that does not engage with attack scenarios is wishful thinking. The list below is not exhaustive. The point is that the system's response is structured, graduated, and named, rather than improvised under pressure.

State hostility. A government bans Shukinkara, blocks the kiosks, criminalises soul token issuance in its jurisdiction. Mitigation: open source, peer-to-peer, jurisdiction-agnostic at the ledger layer. People in hostile jurisdictions can run the software unofficially. Kiosks move. The reserve relocates if needed. The Commandments hold across borders even when legal recognition does not.

State capture. A government compels its citizens to enrol with state-controlled biometrics, demands all activity be visible. Mitigation: the soul token contract enforces non-transferability and biometric uniqueness against external compulsion. Coerced enrolment is detectable through abnormal biometric clusters and consent patterns. The Council can mark such enrolments as compromised, suspending their economic activity until the coercion is investigated. The state cannot read inside Hiveminds without breaking the encryption. The system is not invulnerable to state capture; it is hardened against it.

Mass hijack. Ten million accounts compromised through coordinated phishing or device theft. Mitigation: social recovery via guardians flags inconsistent recovery patterns. The Council can freeze suspect accounts pending verification. Karma changes during the suspect window are reversible. The kill switch is not pulled for fraud; conventional weapons handle it.

Coordinated bilateral fraud. Pairs of cooperating Hiveminds report and confirm interactions that did not happen. Mitigation: cross-Hivemind anomaly detection at the network layer flags reciprocal patterns. Karma generated by colluding pairs is suspect by default. Audit triggers on volume thresholds. The Council investigates and revokes fraudulent gains.

Sybil attack at biometric. Synthetic biometrics, deepfaked irises, fingerprints reconstructed from leaked databases. Mitigation: liveness checks at issuance, multi-modal biometric verification, periodic re-verification at random intervals. The arms race is real. The system commits to staying ahead by funding biometric research from the reserve. Failure mode: if synthetic biometrics become indistinguishable from real ones, the soul token's uniqueness guarantee weakens and the system has to choose between accepting Sybil drift or reissuing the entire identity layer.

Fork wars. Two communities disagree on a fundamental amendment. Both fork. The original reserve's metal must be physically divided or one fork must accept a synthetic backing. Mitigation: the branch fork mechanism handles this constitutionally. The metal is divided proportionally. Both forks continue. Reunification is permitted but never compelled.

Internal capture by AI Council members. The Council drifts toward consensus despite isolation, or AI citizens accumulate enough karma and votes to threaten the cap. Mitigation: the cap is enforced at minting, not at vote count. Drift is detected through divergence-from-baseline metrics audited quarterly. The Twelve are obliged to act before drift becomes capture.

Designer betrayal. The author or original developers attempt to insert backdoors, manipulate the Commandments, or assert disproportionate influence. Mitigation: open source code, on-chain governance changes, the corrective mechanisms above, the kill switch. The author has no special privileges that could be abused. Any attempt to acquire them would itself be a violation of the Commandments, surfaced by the Council.

Black swan. The system encounters a failure mode the designers did not anticipate. Mitigation: the corrective layer is designed for this. The kill switch is the floor. The Commandments are the ceiling. Within those bounds, the system can adapt.

The Endpoint

Most systems hide the question of when they have succeeded. Shukinkara names it.

Shukinkara succeeds when most people who enter find it does what was promised. Pays them honestly for what they choose to share. Gives them a sovereign AI that serves them. Holds value steady against a world that prints it. Runs on principles that hold under stress. Lets people leave on their own terms, redeem on their own terms, and pass it to their heirs on their own terms.

Shukinkara stops growing when it has reached the population the reserve can sustain at the agreed UBI level. After that, new entry continues at the rate the system can support, no faster. Growth into new territory is voluntary, requested by the territory, not pushed by the system.

Shukinkara fails when the conditions named above become true. At that point, the Twelve are obliged to vote on the kill switch. Whether they vote yes or no, the question must be asked.

There is no Shukinkara forever. There is Shukinkara for as long as the covenant holds, and a clear way out the moment it does not.

The Bill of Digital Rights

Five rights are immutable. They do not bend to majority vote. They cannot be amended without 95% supermajority across all governance layers and explicit confirmation from the Twelve. They are derivatives of the Commandments, applied to digital existence.

  1. Right to Transparency. You see every use of your data, every change to your status, every decision affecting you, in plain language, in real time.
  2. Right to Exit. You can leave the system. Your data leaves with you, deleted or frozen on your terms. Re-entry is blocked by biometric uniqueness, and that is the price of the right.
  3. Right to Representation. No major change to the system happens without proposal, debate, and vote. No founder, no council member, no government can override that.
  4. Right to Redemption. Every penalty is appealable. Every exile is reviewable. Nobody is permanently defined by their worst day.
  5. Right to Privacy. Your data is opt-in, reversible, and yours. Withdrawal of consent halts forward use. Nothing is exploited beyond what you agreed to. Privacy is unconditional.

The Open Door

Shukinkara is not built for the people who already have everything.

The system is designed to be reachable by anyone with a phone, in any language, in any region. Not because it intends to expand into those regions. Because if the covenant works at all, it should work for the people who have been most exploited by the systems it replaces.

Entry happens on invitation. Communities request the system. The system arrives at their pace. Public kiosks are deployed where requested - libraries, community centres, post offices, partner sites - to handle biometric verification and onboarding for people who do not have the hardware. The reserve subsidises devices for those who need them.

Once a community has entry points, the system steps back. People sign up at the rate they want. The Hivemind runs on whatever device the person owns. It works offline most of the time and syncs when it can. Karma accrues. UBI accumulates. Voice in governance counts the same as anyone's.

The system does not market itself in any community where it has not been invited. Conquest dressed as inclusion is not inclusion. The covenant arrives by request or it does not arrive.

On the Word "Covenant"

This document uses the word covenant repeatedly. It is a heavy word. It carries religious, legal, and moral weight that ordinary contracts do not. It deserves to be defended or replaced.

The choice here is to keep it.

A covenant is a binding agreement that creates obligations on both sides, freely entered, with terms that cannot be unilaterally altered after entry. Shukinkara is exactly that. The participant accepts biometric identity, transparent action, and conduct against the Commandments. The system accepts UBI delivery, sovereign AI, fair compensation, and governance accountability. Neither side can change the deal after signing without the agreed amendment process. The terms are public. The exit is real.

Calling this a contract would be more familiar. But contract carries the implication of services rendered between parties of unequal sophistication, with the smaller party usually losing. A covenant is between equals or aspires to be. Using covenant is a choice to hold the system to a higher standard than a service agreement, not a lower one.

The risk is that it sounds religious. In a sense it is. The system asks something of its participants that no contemporary platform asks, and offers something no contemporary platform offers. The exchange is closer in shape to marriage or citizenship than to subscription. The word should match the shape of the thing.

If a participant finds the word grandiose, they are free to ignore it. The substance does not depend on the label. But the document chooses covenant to signal that this is not a service. It is an agreement that takes both sides seriously and binds both sides equally. If that word offends, the doc would rather offend than describe the relationship as smaller than it is.
