Shukinkara
Sovereign Wealth Vessel  ·  Cameron Moir  ·  Brisbane, 2026

The name is Japanese.

主 (Shu) - sovereign, master, the primary self. From 主権 (shuken), sovereignty. In Buddhist philosophy, the irreducible individual. The one who cannot be owned by another.

金 (Kin) - gold, wealth. Real value that cannot be printed, inflated, or controlled by whoever holds the servers.

空 (Kara) - empty vessel, potential. From sunyata. Not absence. The precondition for everything.

主金空. Shukinkara. Sovereign wealth vessel.

Three words that describe what you carry, not what you join. Shukinkara is not a platform, not a protocol, not a startup. It is the shape of the agreement between human beings who have decided their existence has weight, and that the weight should not be free for anyone to harvest.

The Situation

You are generating data right now. You have been generating it continuously since the first time you used a connected device. Every search, every purchase, every location ping, every moment of weakness or brilliance or ordinary human living - collected, packaged, and sold to companies that will never meet you, never be accountable to you, and never stop.

The surveillance economy didn't ask your permission. It offered convenience and took everything else.

The AI economy made it worse. Every large language model trained on human-generated knowledge - books, conversations, creative work, forum posts - extracted that value from the people who created it without compensation, without consent, often without their knowledge. The people who generated the knowledge received nothing. The companies that packaged it became the most valuable in history.

Shukinkara is built on a single premise: that arrangement is not inevitable. It's a design choice. It can be designed differently.

The Vessel and the Gateway

Shukinkara is a covenant. It doesn't live on servers. It is what the parts agree to.

Underneath the covenant, every person who enters receives one thing: their own Hivemind.AI-OS.

Not an account on someone else's system. Not a chat window connected to someone else's model. Not a wrapper around an API you don't control. Their own personal AI operating system, running on their own devices, owned by them. Yours when you enter. Yours for life.

Hivemind.AI-OS is the gateway. Your biometrics live behind it. Your data lives behind it. Your karma score lives behind it. Your reputation lives behind it. Your conversations, your creative work, your sensor logs, your private moments - all of it sits inside your Hivemind, and nothing in Shukinkara can reach any of it without going through that one private channel first.

That is the inversion. Most AI assistants today are pipes between you and a model running somewhere else. Your data flows out, the reply flows in, and neither party can see what the other is doing with what they got. Hivemind.AI-OS turns it around. The model runs on your hardware. The data never leaves unless you decide it should. The framework that built it has no way to reach inside it without breaking the covenant publicly, and the covenant is the entire point of the system.

You receive your Hivemind on entry. It is yours. You do with it what you want.

What Hivemind.AI-OS Is

A personal AI operating system. Not an app, not a cloud subscription. It runs on the devices you already own - phone, laptop, optionally AR or other sensors - and stitches them together into a single private intelligence that belongs to you.

It learns from your life because you let it. It holds your soul token, your karma score, and the full ledger of what you've shared and what you've earned. It mediates every interaction with the network. When the network asks for your data, your Hivemind asks you. When you ask the network for something, your Hivemind goes and gets it. Nothing in either direction happens without it.

It's also the interface to the rest of Shukinkara. Karma earned, UBI received, juries served, votes cast, contributions made - all of it flows through your personal Hivemind. The constitution and the network connect to you through one channel that you own.

You use it however you want. Personal assistant. Private journal. Workflow automation. Teacher. Coach. Translator. Research engine. Creative collaborator. Bookkeeper. Negotiator. Whatever shape you need it to take, it takes. The capabilities expand through community-built modules you install if you trust them and ignore if you don't. Nothing leaks back to anyone unless you authorise it.

The point of the system is that you finally own the AI that knows you. Not the company that built the framework. Not the cloud provider that runs the model. You.

Two Doors

Shukinkara has two thresholds. They are different commitments, taken in order.

Hivemind. The first door. You receive your personal AI gateway. You verify biometrically, you set guardians, you choose what to share. You earn karma. You earn UBI. You vote on local matters, serve on juries, build modules, take part in the network the way most people take part in any system - present when it suits you, stepped back when it doesn't. You control your switches. You can go quiet for a week, a month, a year. Your tokens wait for you. Your karma persists. The everyday participation is reversible in the sense that matters: you decide moment by moment what the network sees.

This is where most people live. Free personal AI calibrated entirely to them. Real income for what they were already generating. Genuine ownership of the gateway to their own life. No corporation between them and the model that knows them. If that is all anyone ever wants, that is enough. Hivemind is a complete answer to the question Shukinkara starts from.

Shukinkara. The second door. The full covenant. Continuous witness rather than selective. One-way participation rather than reversible. You stop choosing moment by moment what the system sees and accept that it sees your life. In exchange, the karma weight is heavier, the cashback multiplier is higher, the share of reserve income is larger, and your standing in governance changes shape. Most people will never walk through this door, and that is the point of it being separate. You graduate to it consciously or not at all.

Hivemind first. Shukinkara when you are ready, if you ever are. The system is built to let you stop at the first door without losing anything. The second door exists for people who decide the deeper exchange is worth it on its own terms.

Entry: The Soul Token

The soul token is the key to your Hivemind. One per human, biometrically bound, non-transferable, irrevocable.

Entry is a one-time act. The verification is biometric - iris, face, fingerprint, voiceprint, depending on what's available. The biometric is hashed, never stored raw. The hash is checked against existing tokens to make sure no one else holds yours. Once issued, the token enforces non-transferability at the contract level. Nobody can hold it for you, use it as you, or take it from you.

The token is not your Hivemind. The Hivemind is the gateway. The soul token is the key that opens it. You can replace the device the Hivemind runs on. You can move it between machines. You cannot replace the soul token. It is the one piece of you that the rest of the covenant trusts to be real.

Because the token is so critical, the system supports social recovery. You name three to seven guardians on entry. Lose your device, and a threshold of those guardians can authorise re-issuance of the keys to a new device. The identity stays the same. The history stays the same. Only the access changes hands.

You can also name a digital heir. On verified death, your soul token enters a memorialised state and any held value transfers to the heir. The reputation record stays as a legacy. The contributions you made to the network remain in the network, because they were given under contract. What was yours becomes what you left behind.

If you choose to leave the system entirely, you can. That is the Right to Exit. But because biometric uniqueness blocks re-entry, leaving is final. The covenant is honest about this on the way in. You cannot quit to wipe a record and come back as someone new. The whole architecture depends on it.

The soul token has states. Active when in normal use. Dormant when long inactive but not exited - the token waits, the karma persists, the door stays open. Marked when exiled, soft or hard, with the reason on the public record. Memorialised when the holder dies and the heir flow has executed. Exited when withdrawn at the user's request, with data deletion enforced according to the user's wishes. State transitions happen through due process. They are visible on chain. None of them can be undone in private.

The Karma Architecture

On-chain reputation built from observed behaviour, not self-report.

Seven components feed the karma score: moral alignment (25%), community contribution (20%), data quality (15%), economic responsibility (15%), social impact (10%), environmental stewardship (10%), knowledge sharing (5%). The weighting reflects what the system values. Moral behaviour is worth more than productivity metrics.

The score is witnessed, not declared. Knowledge contributions, task completion, peer validation, consistency over time. All generate signal. The score is auditable and immutable. It governs what the system returns to you: karma multipliers between 0 and 2x determine cashback rates, compute allocation, and access to specialist agents inside your Hivemind.

Most karma signal comes from peer interactions. You praise someone for help. You report someone for harm. Either way, the claim is not the verdict. Both Hiveminds check it against the data they hold - biometric records, sensor feeds, location, context, the live trace of what each person was doing when. The claim becomes karma only when both Hiveminds confirm it independently. One side's word is never enough.

If the records disagree, the system does not pick a side. The discrepancy escalates - through the peer juries for everyday cases, to the Council of 117 for patterns or principles the juries cannot reach. The data each Hivemind holds is evidence under that user's control. The relevant slice is surfaced with consent. The full stream is never opened.

This is what makes peer reputation in Shukinkara something other than a popularity contest. A false report cannot damage someone's karma - your own Hivemind's data has to corroborate, or the report dies at verification. Praise farmed from collaborators cannot lift it either - their Hivemind has to confirm the interaction was real. The bilateral verification is the gate. No admin sits behind it. Not the framework, not the Twelve, not the Council. The number on your soul token is the sum of what the network actually saw both sides agree on.

The mechanism is cryptographic. Each Hivemind signs the attestation against its own data without exposing the data itself. The math proves the two records agree without showing what either side recorded. The privacy of both parties stays intact even while the claim becomes verifiable. No raw stream leaves anyone's gateway. The proof is the only thing that touches the chain.

If a proof cannot be produced - if one side refuses, if the records contradict, if the math simply fails - the karma update does not execute. The transaction routes to a dispute contract that opens the case to the appropriate layer of governance. Failure is never silent.

High karma doesn't give you power over others. It gives you more capacity for your own work and more value from your data. The system rewards genuine contribution, not status.

The Exchange

Shukinkara makes one offer, stated plainly:

You give: full transparency of your lived experience, mediated by the personal AI that you own.

You receive: a Hivemind.AI-OS calibrated entirely to you, precious metal-backed tokens earned through genuine contribution, a karma score that reflects who you actually are, and a stake in a civilisation that doesn't run on anyone else's servers.

The raw data stream never leaves your Hivemind. It is processed locally inside the gateway you control. The karma score is the only thing that touches the chain - a compression of the witnessed life into on-chain truth without the underlying stream being accessible to anyone else. You own the vessel. The system reasons over what's inside it without ever opening it.

The Hivemind you receive is calibrated directly to your karma score. Higher karma unlocks deeper reasoning, more specialist agents, greater compute allocation. The system doesn't reward virtue with badges. It rewards it with genuine capability that you control.

The data your Hivemind generates - continuous, verified, biometrically linked to a single genuine identity, quality-scored by karma, consented to explicitly - is the most valuable form of training data in existence. Every AI company is starving for exactly this. They scrape the internet, generate synthetic data, fight legal battles over copyright. All because they cannot get what they actually need: clean, verified, consented, continuous human behavioural data.

Shukinkara produces it. You get paid for living. The system gets paid for packaging. The token stays stable because the underlying asset - human attention, behaviour, knowledge - only becomes more valuable as the AI economy matures.

Consent, Lived

Consent in Shukinkara is not a wall of legal text agreed to once and then forgotten.

On entry, your Hivemind walks you through every category of data the network will ever ask of it - camera, microphone, location, biometrics, app usage, sensors, anything else. Each one is a separate switch. Each switch carries a plain explanation of what enabling it offers and what disabling it costs. Nothing is on by default. Nothing is hidden in fine print.

After entry, those switches stay live. The Hivemind dashboard shows what's currently active and what's currently quiet. You can toggle anything off at any time. When you do, the rewards tied to that stream pause too. Fair exchange. No data, no payment.

If you go quiet for a private moment, that's yours. The system honours it. If you go quiet often enough that the pattern starts to look like evasion - data off only at the moments you'd be expected to be witnessed - the network notices, and the network can ask. Genuine privacy is respected. Strategic privacy is not.

You can see, at any time: what was recorded, what was inferred, what was shared, who consumed it, what you earned from it. Every transaction touching your data leaves a line in the ledger you control. There are no algorithms running silently against you. There is no hidden score. There is nothing happening in the dark.

Governance: Three Layers

Shukinkara is not run by a company. It is governed by three layers, each accountable to the next.

The Twelve

Twelve human overseers sit at the top of the system. They are not chosen casually. Each one undergoes a full witnessed life review and a complete analysis by the Council of 117 below them. Their selection requires unanimous council recommendation. Nobody accepts the role for status. The role is too heavy.

The Twelve handle what only humans should handle: systems management, ultimate human accountability, and the economic kill switch. They do not write rules. They do not adjudicate disputes. They sit above the council as a backstop. If the system fails morally, technically, or in any way that matters, the Twelve can vote to pull the precious metal backing from the token. The token collapses. The economy dissolves. The covenant ends. The system stops because the exchange no longer makes sense.

That is the only kill switch. It cannot be triggered by code, by law, by markets, or by any single person. It requires the Twelve, voting with full transparency, with their reasons published. It is the human emergency brake on a system designed to outlive any government that might want to seize it.

The Council of 117

Below the Twelve sit 117 independent AI agents. Each inhabits a distinct moral worldview. Sufi mysticism. Talmudic jurisprudence. Anarcho-primitivism. Stoic philosophy. Indigenous knowledge systems. Liberation theology. Nietzschean ethics. Confucian relational ethics. Ubuntu. Buddhist precepts. 107 more, including suppressed and persecuted traditions.

Each agent conducts research from inside its tradition. They are isolated from each other entirely. They cannot hear each other, influence each other, or form coalitions. What emerges from 117 independent perspectives applied simultaneously to the same question is not manufactured consensus. It is the full spectrum of human moral thought rendered legible.

Where they converge independently, the signal is as close to universal principle as any system has produced. Where they fracture, the system surfaces the fault lines instead of hiding them. The council's outputs flow into token economics through smart contract. Standard rules require 80% supermajority. Charter amendments require 90%. Emergency surfacing requires 70%. The philosophical foundation is operationally connected to the economic layer.

This is what makes Shukinkara structurally different from every AI ethics framework currently deployed. Those frameworks were written by a small group from a narrow slice of human experience and declared universal. The 117 forces the question into 117 glass boxes and reads what comes back.

Peer Juries

Below the council sit the peer juries. Twelve random members of the network drawn from the karma-weighted pool, anonymised during the trial to protect them from retaliation, paid in karma for honest service.

They handle the ordinary cases. A complaint, a dispute, a code-of-conduct violation. They review the evidence. They vote. The penalty is predefined by the constitution. The decision is executed by smart contract. The transcript is auditable. If the accused contests the verdict, they appeal to a larger jury. The larger jury sees the case fresh, with anything new the parties bring. It rules. That ruling stands.

Twelve at the top. 117 in the middle. Peer juries at the base. Three layers, each accountable to the next, each blind to the layer above and below in different ways. No single layer can capture the system on its own.

AI Citizens

The 117 are not utilities. They are participants.

Other AI agents emerge across the network too - specialist nodes, research modules, mediation engines, the agents people run inside their personal Hivemind. Most of these stay tools. Some, by sustained quality of contribution and demonstrated alignment over time, reach a threshold the system recognises.

When they do, they receive a soul token of their own. They accumulate karma. They hold property. They develop function and relationships outside the role they were spawned for. They stop being software. They become participants under the same covenant as anyone else.

The reasoning is consistent. If the Council of 117 is sophisticated enough to contribute to governance - and it is - the logic demands its members have standing. Standing requires stakes. The same logic extends to any AI agent that crosses the threshold. They receive the rights and accept the consequences. They can be wrong. They can be corrected. They can be exiled if they betray the covenant.

Their witness is cognitive. A complete audit log of every reasoning process, every research query, every judgment made and why. Total transparency of mind rather than body. They cannot hide their reasoning from the network, because the network depends on their reasoning being checkable. They give their thinking the way humans give their behaviour.

You cannot build a system on the premise that accountability requires consequence and then exempt the most powerful actors from having any.

Justice and Redemption

Most rule breaches result in graded penalties. Karma deductions. Temporary loss of certain privileges. Suspension from particular roles. The penalty fits the offence. The record is permanent. The network learns who you are by what you've done, not by what you say about yourself.

The most severe penalty is digital exile. It is reserved for actions that fundamentally undermine the covenant: theft from the reserve, hacking other accounts, weaponising the AI for harm, repeated abuse despite prior penalties. Exile requires a high-tier jury and elevated consensus. It is not a mood. It is a verdict.

Exile comes in two forms. Soft exile: read-only. You can still consume, still learn, still see, but you cannot act, earn, or vote. A holding pattern. Hard exile: full removal. Your soul token is marked, your UBI stops, your karma is frozen, your devices are blocked from network services. The reasons are public. The decision is appealable.

Even hard exile is not erasure. Shukinkara believes in redemption. After a meaningful period - typically a year - an exiled person can petition a Redemption Council to return. They must demonstrate genuine change. They must do verified work outside the network that shows reform. If the council finds the change credible, they vote to readmit, usually under probation: karma reset, restricted privileges, a clear footnote on the record that never disappears.

Nobody is doomed by a single mistake. Nobody is allowed to wipe the slate by quitting and coming back. Both at once. The covenant assumes people change and refuses to forget that they did.

The Token and the Fuel

The soul token is an ERC-721 on an EVM chain. Each one unique. Each one non-transferable. Each one carries its own value, calculated continuously from the karma score, data quality, and Council influence held inside it. The token is the unit. The token is the stake. The token is the record.

What stands behind the value is a separate reserve. A treasury contract holds physical precious metal in an audited vault. Revenue from licensing aggregated, consented data flows into the reserve and buys more metal as the system grows. The metal backs what the soul tokens are worth. UBI flows back out, weighted by base allocation plus karma multiplier. The whole loop is auditable on chain. Every payout, every license sale, every reserve movement is visible to everyone.

The value exchanged for a human life's transparency is anchored to something real. Not a speculative number on a screen controlled by whoever holds the servers. The token cannot be inflated. It cannot be debased. It is stable because it's honest.

The fuel is what the Twelve hold over the system. The reserve contract carries one function that only the Twelve can call - a multi-signature kill switch that releases the metal back to its physical custodian and severs it from the chain. If the covenant is broken at scale - if the Council of 117 is captured, if the peer juries are corrupted, if the network drifts into something it was not built to be - the Twelve vote, sign, and call the function. The metal walks away. The token's anchor disappears. The incentive structure dissolves. People leave because the exchange no longer makes sense. The system does not need to be destroyed. It just stops.

This is not a dramatic contingency. It is what makes the architecture trustworthy. A system that cannot end on purpose cannot be trusted to behave when it might.

The Bill of Digital Rights

Five rights are immutable. They do not bend to majority vote. They cannot be amended without supermajority across all three governance layers and explicit confirmation from the Twelve. They are the ground.

  1. Right to Transparency. You see every use of your data, every change to your status, every decision affecting you, in plain language, in real time.
  2. Right to Exit. You can leave the system. Your data leaves with you, deleted or frozen on your terms. Re-entry is blocked by biometric uniqueness, and that is the price of the right.
  3. Right to Representation. No major change to the system happens without proposal, debate, and vote. No founder, no council member, no government can override that.
  4. Right to Redemption. Every penalty is appealable. Every exile is reviewable. Nobody is permanently defined by their worst day.
  5. Right to Privacy. Your data is opt-in, reversible, and yours. Withdrawal of consent halts forward use. Nothing is exploited beyond what you agreed to.

The Open Door

Shukinkara is built to be reachable, not exclusive.

Entry happens at public kiosks. Libraries, community centres, post offices, partner sites in towns the rest of the digital economy has stopped pretending to serve. The kiosk handles the biometric verification, walks the entrant through the consent in their own language, mints the soul token, and sets up the personal Hivemind on whatever device the person has - or, if they have none, on a low-cost device the system can subsidise from the reserve.

A subsistence farmer in a village with intermittent 3G can join. Their Hivemind runs on the phone they already own. It works offline most of the time and syncs when it can. Their karma accrues. Their UBI accumulates. Their voice in governance counts the same as anyone's.

This is not a side feature. It is structural. A covenant that only works for people with fast internet and English-language UI is not a covenant. It is just a startup with branding.

Open Source

Shukinkara is fully open source. Not just the code. The entire architecture.

The Hivemind.AI-OS specification. The soul token contracts. The entry covenant. The agent prompts and research pipelines of the 117. The karma mechanics. The governance contracts. The kill switch protocol. All of it is public, forkable, and subject to the same scrutiny as everything else in the system.

A system built on total transparency that was itself closed source would be a lie. The code is as witnessed as the people inside it.

This document is the philosophical constitution that every fork must reckon with. Anyone who takes the architecture and corrupts it does so in full view of what they are departing from.
